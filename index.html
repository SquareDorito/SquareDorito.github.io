About Us

Who We Are

Schools for Africa Fund is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization that strives to bring sustainable eduction to children and communities in impoverished regions of Africa.

Our Mission

Our goal is to aid these children in escaping the shackles of poverty by providing them with a brighter future through education and innovation.

Meet the Founder

Eleanor Su

The concept of Schools for Africa Fund was ideated by Eleanor Su when she was ten years old. In 2009, Eleanor first learned about the Sierra Leone civil war through Free the Children’s California Take Action Academy. She was deeply touched by a particular image of a young Sierra Leonean boy who had been a victim of the conflict--one of his arms had been severed by a machete. She was shocked to discover that due to this conflict, communities and schools were destroyed and thousands of children were forced to become children soldiers.

This moment inspired Eleanor to take action and work towards making a difference in the lives of these children. Since 2009, Schools for Africa Fund has raised more than $56,000 to build four schools in Makeni, Sierra Leone through its international partners. The organization fundraises primarily through local events such as annual benefit concerts, bake sales, concessions stands, and run-a-thons, as well as through grants and corporate sponsors. Additionally, in 2013, Eleanor traveled to Kenya and worked on the foundation of Oleleshwa Secondary School through the organization Free the Children. She represented Schools for Africa Fund as a 2015 Global Teen Leader at the Three Dot Dash Summit in New York.

Impact


Schools for Africa Fund strives to empower North American youth to make a difference in the lives of others in developing countries. Through our various fundraisers and awareness-raising events, we work towards with our international partners on our projects to impact communities at large in impoverished regions of Africa, particularly the Makeni community of Sierra Leone and the Darfur region in Sudan. To ensure transparency, we frequently update our audiences with detailed reports and pictures of our ongoing and past projects.

History

Why Education?

Providing schools with safe environments allows the students to gain a basic education and the technical skills to enable sustainable livelihoods and lead productive lives. Over a decade after the war, the Makeni community is still severely neglected by the Sierra Leonean government. Thus, Schools for Africa Fund strives to raise awareness about the situation in Sierra Leone as well as raise funds to provide the children with sustainable educations.

Education provides these disadvantaged children with the voice to speak out and a plethora of opportunities for a brighter future. Education is the key to halting this cycle of poverty, as the mere ability to read and write has the potential to lift thousands out of poverty. Schools would provide not only widespread literacy, but also a better understanding of basic sanitation practices and other crucial skills.

Post-war Recovery

After Eleanor returned from Kenya, she realized something was missing beyond the basic education provided in classrooms-something that she had learned from growing up in the Silicon Valley: entrepreneurship. Schools for Africa Fund has since provided opportunities such as mentorship, seed funding, design workshops for rapid prototyping.

The ebola outbreak in West Africa shut down schools in Sierra Leone for nine months, but schools reopened on April 14, 2015. Despite the shutdown, the communities in which the projects were implemented demonstrated incredible resilience. To combat the shutdown, Schools for Africa distributed toolkits in the areas of electronics, digital media, and crafts. From simple kits demonstrating the science of capacitance and resistance to holding regional design challenges, these at-home labs have since provided the capacity for future innovators to expand their creative outlook.
